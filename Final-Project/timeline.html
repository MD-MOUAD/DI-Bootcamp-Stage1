The Journey of a Legend

A chronological look at Cristiano Ronaldo's remarkable career

Career Timeline

1985

The Beginning

Born on February 5, 1985, in Funchal, Madeira, Portugal to Maria Dolores dos Santos and José Dinis Aveiro.

1992

First Steps in Football

Joined amateur team Andorinha, where his father worked as a kit man.

1997

Moving to Lisbon

Joined Sporting CP's youth academy after a successful three-day trial.

2002

Professional Debut

Made his professional debut for Sporting CP at the age of 17.

2003

Manchester United Transfer

Signed by Manchester United for £12.24 million after impressing Sir Alex Ferguson in a friendly match.

2004

International Recognition

Represented Portugal at Euro 2004, helping them reach the final on home soil.

2008

First Ballon d'Or

Won his first Ballon d'Or after scoring 42 goals in all competitions and helping Manchester United win the Champions League.

2009

World Record Transfer

Transferred to Real Madrid for a then-world record fee of £80 million.

2013-2014

La Décima

Helped Real Madrid win their long-awaited 10th Champions League title. Won his second Ballon d'Or.

2016

European Champion

Led Portugal to victory in Euro 2016, their first major international trophy despite getting injured in the final.

2018

Juventus Transfer

After winning his fifth Champions League with Real Madrid, transferred to Juventus for €100 million.

2021

Return to Manchester

Made a sensational return to Manchester United, scoring twice on his second debut.

2022

800+ Career Goals

Became the first player in football history to score 800+ career goals.

2023

Al Nassr Move

Signed with Saudi Arabian club Al Nassr on a 2.5-year contract, becoming one of the highest-paid athletes in the world.

Present

The Legacy Continues

Continuing to break records and inspire millions of fans around the world while approaching the twilight of his illustrious career.

Notable Records

  • All-time top goalscorer in UEFA Champions League history
  • Portugal's all-time top goalscorer and most-capped player
  • First player to score in five different World Cup tournaments
  • Real Madrid's all-time top goalscorer
  • Most goals in a UEFA Champions League season (17 in 2013-14)
  • Only player to score 50+ goals in seven consecutive seasons
  • Most followed person on Instagram
  • First football player to become a billionaire
